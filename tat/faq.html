Question 01

Does Getting a Tattoo Actually Hurt?

Honestly? Yes — but probably not as much as you're expecting. The sensation is most commonly described as a "hot scratching" feeling. The level of discomfort varies significantly depending on placement. Areas with more fat and fewer nerve endings (outer arm, thigh, calf) are generally more tolerable than bonier spots like the ribs, spine, or hands and feet.

Our artists are experienced in making the experience as comfortable as possible. We take breaks when needed, work at a pace that suits you, and maintain a calm, light-hearted studio environment. Most clients walk out saying it was way easier than they expected.

Question 02

How Much Does a Tattoo Cost at Inkwave?

Pricing varies based on size, complexity, placement, and the specific artist you're booking. As a general guide:

  • Small (under 5cm): From R 800
  • Medium (5–15cm): From R 1,500
  • Large (15cm+): From R 2,500
  • Half sleeve: From R 6,000
  • Full sleeve: From R 12,000

All pricing is discussed and confirmed during your free consultation — there are no hidden fees or surprises. A deposit (typically 30%) is required to secure your booking and is deducted from the final total.

Question 03

How Do I Prepare for My Tattoo Appointment?

Preparation makes a real difference to both how the tattoo sits in the skin and how comfortable the session is. Here's what we recommend:

  • Eat a substantial meal within 2 hours of your appointment.
  • Stay well-hydrated — drink extra water the day before and on the day.
  • Get a good night's sleep the night before.
  • Wear (or bring) clothing that provides easy access to the area being tattooed.
  • Avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours before your session.
  • Don't apply any lotions or creams to the area on the day.
  • If you're nervous, let your artist know — we can talk you through it.

Question 04

How Long Will My Session Take?

Session length depends on the complexity and size of the design, the placement, and the individual's pain tolerance (which affects the number and length of breaks needed).

As a rough guide: small tattoos usually take 1–2 hours, medium pieces 2–4 hours, and larger work can require multiple sessions of 4–6 hours each. We always build in adequate time — we will not rush a tattoo, ever.

For larger projects like sleeves or back pieces, we schedule multiple sessions spaced 4–6 weeks apart to allow proper healing between sittings.

Question 05

How Do I Look After My New Tattoo?

Aftercare is crucial for both the health of your skin and the longevity of your tattoo. We provide every client with a written aftercare guide and a starter kit. The basics are:

  • Keep covered with cling wrap or second skin for 2–4 hours post-session.
  • Wash gently twice daily with unscented antibacterial soap.
  • Apply a thin layer of unscented moisturiser 3× daily (we recommend Hustle Butter).
  • Do not submerge in water for 3 weeks.
  • Avoid direct sun exposure and apply SPF50+ on healed tattoos.
  • Do NOT pick, scratch, or peel the healing skin.

Question 06

Can You Cover Up an Old or Unwanted Tattoo?

Absolutely — cover-ups are something we genuinely love and excel at. Our experienced artists will assess your existing tattoo (size, darkness, colour) and discuss realistic options with you. Not every tattoo can be completely disguised with a single session, but we always give you an honest assessment.

In some cases, a few sessions of professional laser tattoo removal before a cover-up can open up significantly more design options. We can refer you to trusted laser clinics in the area if needed.

Book a consultation and bring your old tattoo in — we'll figure out the best path forward together.

Question 07

What Do I Need to Know About Getting Pierced?

Our certified piercer Aisha performs all piercings using single-use, sterile needles and implant-grade titanium or solid gold jewellery — never guns, never mystery metals.

Before your appointment:

  • Eat beforehand to stabilise your blood sugar.
  • Avoid alcohol for 24 hours prior.
  • Clean the area gently — don't apply heavy products.
  • Bring valid ID (required for all piercing clients).

Healing times vary by placement — ear lobes take around 6 months, cartilage can take up to a year, and body piercings can take 12–18 months to fully heal. We'll walk you through everything specific to your piercing.

Question 08

What Is Your Policy for Clients Under 18?

In accordance with South African law, we require all clients to be 18 years or older to receive a tattoo — no exceptions, regardless of parental consent.

For piercings, clients aged 16–17 may be pierced with the written and in-person consent of a parent or legal guardian. Clients under 16 may receive ear lobe piercings only with a parent or guardian present. Valid ID is required for the client and the consenting adult.

We take this policy seriously and ID will always be requested if there is any doubt about age. We appreciate your understanding.
