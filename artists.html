Meet Our Artists

Four extraordinary creatives. One shared passion for exceptional body art.

Lead Artist · 7 Years Experience

Zara Khumalo

Born and raised on the KwaZulu-Natal coast, Zara trained under a master tattooist in Cape Town before returning to Ballito to open Inkwave. Her work is rooted in precision — every line is intentional, every element considered. Outside the studio, she's a passionate surfer and amateur botanical illustrator, both of which you'll see reflected in her art.

Geometric Neo-Traditional Blackwork Fine Line

Fine Line Specialist · 5 Years Experience

Luca de Beer

After studying graphic design in Johannesburg, Luca discovered his true calling behind a tattoo machine. He's obsessed with the interplay between negative space and ultra-thin linework — creating pieces that look as though they were drawn directly onto skin with an HB pencil. His botanical and portrait work has been featured in Tattoo Life Magazine.

Fine Line Botanical Portraits Minimalist

Colour & Realism Specialist · 6 Years Experience

Aisha Naidoo

Aisha spent three years apprenticing under one of Durban's most celebrated colour artists before joining the Inkwave family. She specialises in vibrant, photorealistic colour work — her sleeves are legendary along the KZN coast. Aisha is also Inkwave's certified piercer, bringing the same precision and care to jewellery placement as she does to ink.

Colour Realism Sleeves Watercolour Body Piercing
Book with Aisha
Ig @aisha.tattoos TikTok

Traditional & Japanese Specialist · 9 Years Experience

Marco Swart

Marco is the veteran of the Inkwave crew — with nine years behind the machine and stints in tattoo studios in Tokyo, Amsterdam, and New York. His traditional Japanese Irezumi work is unrivalled in the province, and his Old-School American pieces carry the bold confidence of someone who has perfected their craft over thousands of hours. If you want something built to last generations, Marco is your man.

Japanese / Irezumi Old School American Neo-Traditional Cover-Ups
